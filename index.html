Question 2 · answer

Four patterns. All four are execution, not concept.

Answer first: every low-S2S ad shares one or more of these four patterns — product-line concentration, unsupported outcome framing, non-testimonial format fatigue, and over-reliance on a single voice persona. None of these are about which motivator or hook archetype to use. They're about how those inputs are executed.

01

Concentrated on two product lines

3-in-1 Pill + Sex Rx + Testosterone account for the majority of low-S2S spend despite being a minority of total portfolio spend. The same physical offer framing (triple-stack outcome promise) appears disproportionately in the low cluster.

Evidence loading…
02

Outcome-framed hooks executed without credibility rails

M-OUTCOME hooks dominate the low cluster but underperform the same motivator in the high cluster. The issue is not motivator choice — it's the delivery: unsupported outcome claims ("twice as hard," "17 times a month") landing without the earned proof beats that the Climax Control creative uses (Cialis® equivalence, clinical citations, husband-voice framing).

Evidence loading…
03

Hybrid Product Feature Callout format fatigue

Non-testimonial formats (Hybrid Product Feature Callout, Presenter-led voiceover, Produced Infomercial) average substantially lower S2S than UGC Testimonial Video, and those formats skew heavily into 3-in-1 Pill and Sx+Testo. Direct-to-camera testimonial outperforms when the product line is credible; it doesn't rescue triple-stack offers.

Evidence loading…
04

Over-concentration on VP-F2534 (young female voice)

A large share of low-cluster spend runs with VP-F2534 (female, 25–34, speaking about partner). When paired with weaker outcome framing, the 20-something wife persona compounds skepticism for a buyer who skews older. The high cluster uses more balanced persona mix and tighter proof choreography.
